About Me

Currently

I lead cyber safety policy development and enforcement at Anthropic, where I'm building our cyber harm constitution, developing monitoring and streaming classifiers, and creating enforcement guidelines. My work focuses on developing AI-enabled cyber defense capabilities and supporting civil society through spyware detection and censorship filtering projects.

Recent work includes:

  • CAMLIS 2024 - Presented on large language models and threat intelligence
  • IEEE AI x Cyber - Contributing to the intersection of AI and cybersecurity
  • CAMLIS 2025 - Upcoming research on threat actor attribution using LLMs

Previously

I recently completed my MS in Computer Science from Stanford, where my thesis focused on LLMs and threat intelligence. During my time there, I co-founded the GirlCon Tech Conference, now in its 8th year, bringing together 900 virtual and 200 in-person high school students annually.

Before Anthropic, I gained experience across various technical domains - from building robots and developing ML models for national security at Vannevar Labs, to creating simulation software at NVIDIA, leading AV fleet deployment research at Cruise, and flying drones at Zipline.
